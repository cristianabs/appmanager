FROM CONCEPT TO REALITY: THE EVOLUTION OF THRADEX'S MULTI-VENDOR NETWORK MANAGEMENT

THE ORIGINS

In the lead-up to the 2014 FIFA World Cup, Brazil faced the challenge of ensuring seamless communication and connectivity across all event venues. Embratel, a leading Brazilian telecommunications provider, was tasked with building a state-of-the-art network infrastructure to deliver high-speed internet, broadcasting, and telecom services. To meet the significant data demands and ensure uninterrupted connectivity, Embratel partnered with Ciena, a global leader in networking systems.

In 2013, Ciena awarded Thradex a contract to establish the Network Operations Centre (NOC) for Embratel’s network. Given the complexity of the task, Thradex developed AppManager—a software platform designed to optimize network operations. The platform offered key functionalities, including real-time network inventory, alarm management, and performance monitoring of optical transport links. AppManager's deployment was crucial for maintaining smooth operations during the tournament and managing the complex network infrastructure.

INITIAL DEVELOPMENT

AppManager was initially tailored to support the World Cup, with core functionalities focused on:

• Network Inventory Management: Real-time monitoring of network components and configurations.
• Alarm Management: Detecting and resolving network issues promptly.
• Performance Monitoring: Tracking transport link performance to ensure optimal network operation.

These capabilities played a vital role in maintaining the network’s reliability throughout the event.

EXPANDING CAPABILITIES

Following the success of the World Cup, Thradex expanded AppManager's features to meet increasing client needs. The platform became an indispensable tool for Thradex’s deployment and technical support teams, who utilized it for ongoing network management and operational improvements.

A significant focus was enhancing AppManager’s integration capabilities. Thradex introduced support for various protocols like CORBA, REST, TL1, and SNMP, enabling the platform to seamlessly interact with other network management systems and adapt to different networking environments.

TAPI SUPPORT

To support modern network management requirements, Thradex aligned AppManager with industry standards, including the Open Networking Foundation (ONF) Transport API (TAPI). Telefonica’s leadership in driving multi-vendor and multi-operator interoperability influenced this evolution, drawing insights from initiatives like the OIF 2020 Transport SDN API Interop Demo and the Multi-Vendor, Multi-Operator TAPI Testing hosted at Telefónica’s Madrid Lab.  Telefonica remains instrumental in shaping ONF TAPI and Telecom Infra Project (TIP) Open Optical & Packet Transport (OOPT) initiatives.

INTEGRATION & INTEROPERABILITY

• RESTCONF and YANG Models: AppManager was upgraded to support these protocols, essential for TAPI. This advancement enhanced the platform's ability to communicate with Software-Defined Networking (SDN) controllers and other management systems, improving automation and interoperability.

• Multi-Layer Support: The platform’s capabilities were extended to manage various network layers, including photonic (Layer 0), Optical Transport Network (Layer 1), and Ethernet (Layer 2), ensuring comprehensive multi-technology management.

FOLLOWING THE STANDARDS

Telefonica's involvement in TAPI development and testing ensured AppManager addressed real-world operational requirements, guiding its evolution.

• Interoperability Testing: Lessons from testing events hosted by Telefonica and other industry leaders validated AppManager's compatibility with TAPI, reinforcing its integration into multi-vendor environments.

ENHANCED DATA EXCHANGE

• Unified Data Model: By adopting a TAPI-compatible unified data model, AppManager facilitated efficient data exchange across various management systems, ensuring consistent and accurate information sharing.

• Real-Time Synchronization: The platform's ability to synchronize data in real time provided up-to-date network status and performance metrics across systems, ensuring smooth operations.

USER INTERFACE ENHANCEMENTS

• Integrated Dashboards: AppManager's interface was improved with dashboards displaying data from TAPI and TIP OOPT-compliant systems, offering network operators a holistic view of network health in real time.

• Customizable Views: Users could personalize their dashboards to highlight relevant metrics, enhancing usability and network management efficiency.

Through these advancements, Thradex positioned AppManager as a versatile solution for managing complex, multi-vendor network environments. Its alignment with ONF TAPI and TIP OOPT standards, combined with insights from Telefonica’s contributions, played a crucial role in AppManager’s evolution, ensuring its relevance in the rapidly changing telecommunications landscape.
